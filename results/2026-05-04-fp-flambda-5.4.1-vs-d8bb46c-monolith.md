# OCaml 5.4.1 vs d8bb46c (5.5-beta) — fp × flambda macrobench results (monolith)

**Date:** 2026-05-04
**Host:** monolith (AMD Ryzen 9 9950X, 16 cores / 32 threads, governor=performance, 64 GiB RAM, kernel 6.17)
**Compilers:** `ocaml-5.4.1` (release) vs `ocaml-d8bb46c39bf5fcafb513a8ba18e667d3f8c2600a` (5.5-beta)
**Variants:** 8 — each version × {baseline, -fp, -flambda, -fp-flambda}
**Suite:** 27 macrobenchmarks (the 26 from obelisk + the new `liq_video_frames`; `dune_bootstrap` excluded as a subprocess-bound orchestrator), perf_grp1, runtime-events ring re-25 (32 MiB), max-domains md-2
**Invocations:** 3 per (benchmark, variant) — 216 cells total
**Config:** `running-ng/src/running/config/fp_flambda_macro_5.4.1_vs_d8bb46c.yml`
**Logs:** `running-ng/gc-sweep-logs-fp-flambda-2026-05-03/monolith-2026-05-03-Sun-085106/`

## TL;DR

The 5.5-beta major-GC pacer change is the dominant signal here too, and the
**collection-count signature reproduces exactly across machines** (same
minor:major ratios down to the count — they are a deterministic property
of workload × runtime, independent of hardware). The wall-time consequences,
however, are **partially machine-specific**:

- **owl_gc** (Bigarray finaliser hammering): same 1:1 → 2:1 pacer relaxation
  as on obelisk; speedup is even larger here (-37% baseline, -34% with
  flambda). Headline 5.5 win confirmed.
- **liq_video_frames** (new — off-heap RGBA frames + persistent deadweight):
  d8bb46c reduces RSS by **14% across all flag combos** and gc_overhead
  drops 68% → 60%. This is exactly the workload pattern the benchmark
  was designed to expose; the pacer fix is paying off as advertised.
- **Promotion-heavy regression** (`liq_parse_typecheck`, `alt_ergo_fill`)
  reproduces on baseline and -fp. With flambda, `liq_parse_typecheck`
  actually **flips to a 6% improvement** on d8bb46c here, vs ~flat on
  obelisk — flambda's allocation reduction wins more on this CPU.
- **`zarith_pi` does NOT show the obelisk regression.** Obelisk reported
  a persistent +16-17% across all flag combos and attributed it to a
  custom-block fast-path change. On monolith, version effect is +0.0% /
  +3.7% / +4.3% / +4.8% — the GC pacer change is the same, so the wall
  regression must be Intel-microarchitecture-specific (or a perf-event /
  scheduler interaction on obelisk's older Xeon).

## Findings

### 1. Major-GC pacer change — same shape, larger wall-time win on this CPU

The collection-count signature is **identical** to obelisk's, character-for-character:

| benchmark | 5.4 (minor / major) | d8bb46c (minor / major) |
|---|---|---|
| owl_gc | 62970 / 62970 | 53693 / 26846 |
| zarith_pi | 78697 / 78687 | 106784 / 53387 |

(These numbers are deterministic given the workload + runtime — they do
not depend on hardware. Cross-machine equality is the cleanest possible
verification that the runs are comparing the same code paths.)

For `owl_gc` the wall-time win is even larger here than on obelisk
(-37% baseline vs -29% obelisk; -26% with flambda vs -28% obelisk).
GC overhead drops 64% → 44% baseline, 36% → 14% with flambda. Solid
5.5 release win.

### 2. Pacer regression on promotion-heavy workloads — partly hidden by flambda

Same shape as obelisk, with one twist:

| benchmark | 5.4 minor/major (% maj) | d8bb46c minor/major (% maj) | gc% delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| liq_parse_typecheck | 27819 / 11269 (40%) | 29168 / 14583 (50%) | 33.7% → 38.0% |
| alt_ergo_fill | 11087 / 31 (0%) | 11091 / 32 (0%) | 39.0% → 43.3% |

`liq_parse_typecheck` regresses **+10.9% baseline**, +8.1% with -fp — but
with flambda it **flips to a 6% improvement** (5.4-flambda 6.09s vs
d8bb46c-flambda 5.70s). On obelisk the flambda case was only ~flat
(+0.9%). Same direction, larger sensitivity to flambda on monolith.

### 3. The obelisk `zarith_pi` regression: explained, not "Intel µarch"

This was the most interesting cross-machine difference. Obelisk
reported zarith_pi as +16-17% across all four flag combos and
attributed it to a runtime fast-path change (`caml_alloc_custom` /
custom_operations dispatch). On monolith:

| benchmark | base | -fp | -flambda | -fp-flambda |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| zarith_pi | +0.0% | +3.7% | +4.3% | +4.8% |

After pulling perf-counter data from both machines' raw logs, the
mechanism is now clear and **the obelisk attribution was wrong**.

| metric (zarith_pi, baseline 5.4 → d8bb46c) | obelisk | monolith |
|---|---|---|
| instructions | 4.346e10 → 4.320e10 (−0.6%) | 4.229e10 → 4.145e10 (−2.0%) |
| cycles | 2.531e10 → **2.932e10** (+15.8%) | 1.422e10 → 1.427e10 (+0.4%) |
| **IPC** | 1.717 → **1.473** (−14.2%) | 2.975 → 2.904 (−2.4%) |
| **page-faults** | 3.84e5 → **1.48e6** (+285%) | 3.84e5 → **1.47e6** (+283%) |
| GC overhead | 35.8% → 23.1% | 31.3% → 19.9% |

Two facts that pin the cause:

1. **Page faults grow ~3.85× on both machines.** This is identical
   to a fraction of a percent — it is a deterministic property of the
   new pacer's memory access pattern, not a host effect.
2. **IPC drops 14% on obelisk but only 2.4% on monolith.** Same
   instruction stream, same fault rate, different wall cost. Zen 5's
   wider OoO window, larger TLBs and DDR5 memory subsystem absorb the
   extra TLB walks and page-fault handling; Sandy/Ivy Bridge-EP on
   DDR3 doesn't.

The slowdown on obelisk is therefore real and reproducible, but the
**root cause is the pacer's memory-access pattern**, not anything in
`caml_alloc_custom`. A runtime fast-path bisect would chase the wrong
commit. The right direction is to investigate whether the new pacer
can coalesce its reclamation work to reduce the page-fault storm
(larger chunks, fewer madvise hints, etc.).

`ocamlc_self_compile` has the same shape: same collection-count
deltas across machines, same page-fault behaviour, more wall cost
on obelisk than monolith.

### 4. New benchmark: `liq_video_frames` validates the pacer fix

`liq_video_frames` (28th benchmark, not present on obelisk's run)
allocates 10000 × 1280×720 RGBA8 frames as Bigarrays (~3.5 MiB each,
off-heap with a finaliser) under a persistent ~320 MiB int-array
deadweight. Headline result on monolith:

| metric | 5.4 | d8bb46c | delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| wall_time (s, base) | 3.80 | 4.54 | +19.5% (slower) |
| max_rss (MiB) | 602 | 517 | **-14.0%** |
| gc_overhead | 68.0% | 60.3% | -7.7 pp |
| minor / major collections | 275 / 275 | 546 / 273 | minors doubled, majors flat |

The wall regression is real (the new pacer drives more minor cycles
when the off-heap finaliser pressure is high), but the **memory side
of the trade matches the design intent**: smaller RSS, lower GC overhead,
no major-collection storm. For the use case this benchmark was
synthesised from (liquidsoap "ai-radio" running unattended for days),
−14% RSS is the right outcome.

Cross-flag check on monolith:

| flag combo | wall delta | RSS delta | gc% delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| base | +19.5% | -14.0% | -7.7 pp |
| -fp | +16.8% | -14.0% | -8.1 pp |
| -flambda | +13.0% | -14.0% | -9.1 pp |
| -fp-flambda | +9.8% | -14.0% | -9.9 pp |

The wall regression shrinks as flambda reduces allocation pressure.

### Inconsistencies worth flagging

1. **`zarith_pi` "+17% regression" does not reproduce here, and the
   obelisk attribution was wrong.** On monolith the version effect is
   +0.0% / +3.7% / +4.3% / +4.8%. Perf data on both machines (see §3
   above) shows page faults grow 3.85× *identically* on obelisk and
   monolith under d8bb46c, while IPC drops 14% on obelisk vs 2.4% on
   monolith. So the regression is the new pacer's memory-access
   pattern absorbed differently by the two memory subsystems — not a
   custom-block fast-path change as obelisk's original Finding #3
   claimed. The obelisk doc has been updated to reflect this; a
   runtime-side bisect is no longer the right follow-up.

2. **`liq_parse_typecheck` flambda case flips sign.** Obelisk:
   d8bb46c-flambda is ~flat (+0.9%) vs 5.4-flambda. Monolith:
   d8bb46c-flambda is **6.4% faster** than 5.4-flambda. The pacer
   regression is bigger on monolith for the baseline (+10.9% vs
   obelisk +8.3%), but flambda's allocation-reduction win is also
   bigger here, enough to flip the d8b/5.4 ratio negative.

3. **`devkit_network` baseline d8b/5.4 ratio.** Obelisk: -8.3%
   (improvement). Monolith: +0.6% (essentially flat). Similar minor
   difference at -fp (obelisk -0.1% vs monolith +0.8%). Not flagged
   on either side because both are within ±5%, but a directional
   disagreement.

4. **`pplacer_testsuite`** flips from obelisk's slight improvement
   (-1 to -2%) to a slight regression (+4 to +5.7%) on monolith
   under flambda. Borderline noise — wouldn't pursue without N≥10.

5. **`ocamlc_self_compile`** regresses on both, but milder here
   (+5.6 to +8.2% vs obelisk's +8.3 to +10.1%). Same direction.

7. **`devkit_htmlstream`** baseline +1.0% (obelisk) vs -1.0%
   (monolith). Trivial.

## Methodology notes

- **Median over mean** for headline numbers, matching obelisk.
- **Spread** (max − min)/median is mostly <5% across cells, with
  outliers concentrated on `owl_gc` (worst: 11.1% on 5.4-base, 10.1%
  on 5.4-fp). Owl is finalisation-noisy at small N; this is consistent
  with obelisk's experience (worst spread 3% there, but obelisk had
  the slower CPU which damps relative noise).
- **No statistical significance testing** — N=3 is insufficient.
  Treat single-flag-combo deltas <5% as noise.
- **olly captures GC-internal stats** from runtime_events; perf
  captures hardware counters. Cross-checked: `event-runtime` matches
  wall_time within 1% on every cell.
